About

ReDisulphID is a bioinformatics tool that uses available structural data to predict the presence of redox-active intermolecular disulphide bonds in proteins.

This has been achieved by searching all mammalian protein structures for cysteine pairs with thiols within 10 Å of each other in the RCSB protein databank. Finally, known redox-active disulphides were used to determine parameters for prediction of further such disulphides.

HSE - Half sphere exposure

HSE is a measure of the accessibility of an amino acid side-chain. Cα atoms are counted in a 12 &# ; radius around the Cα of each cysteine in the potential redox-active disulphide bond. A lower HSE means an amino acid has fewer proximal amino acids, so is more accessible.

Redox Score

The redox score for each potential redox-active disulphide is calculated from factors including the HSE and distance between each thiol in the cysteine pair. The highest redox score is 1.82, and the lowest redox score of a known redox-active disulphide is 0.25, while the lowest score overall is 0.1.

Duplicates

A particular cysteine pair often features in multiple structures, which would lead to duplicates. To remove duplicates, cysteines were assigned to a residue number in their UniProt sequence, then the structure where the cysteine thiols are closest to each other is chosen for that potential redox-active disulphide.

Exclusions

Some structures or cystein pairs have been excluded. Reasons include:
  • The distance between the thiols in the pair was too short (< 1.7 Å) to be considered real.
